Listed below are a plethora of great resources! Whether you're passionate about books, movies or TV shows, you can find plenty of links to interesting websites which provide reviews, information or the ability to watch or read your favourites.

Movies and TV shows

Websites:

Netflix

Netflix is the world's most popular entertainment service where you can watch all your favourite TV shows and movies online and they have a free 30 day trial.

IMDb

IMDb is the most information-packed website you can find on movies and TV shows. From top-rated shows and movies, movies coming soon, showtimes & tickets to India TV Spotlight and celebrity news, you'll find exactly what you're looking for.

Amazon prime video

Amazon Prime Video lets you watch all of the most popular movies and TV shows as well as providing a free one-day delivery on millions of items. You can watch offline on the Prime Video app when you download movies/shows. They provide a 30-day free trial and it costs Â£7.99/month after the trial.

ITV

Watch all of the most recent shows on ITV channels online for free.

Metacritic

Metacritic provides reviews, trailers, articles and more on movies and TV shows.

Collider

Collider provides news on movies and TV shows, articles such as the best movies and shows on Netflix, Movie talk, reviews, trailers and more.

Apps:

Cinema-Finder

The Cinema-Finder app allows you to find cinemas near you or anywhere in the world.

Pluto TV

Pluto TV is a popular free movie apps which offers 100s of TV channels and 1,000s of movies and TV shows on-demand.

Snagfilms

If you love documentaries or classic movies, Snagfilms is the best free streaming app. It has a dark theme and it allows you to download movies for offline streaming. It's available on both iOS and Android

Books

Websites:

Goodreads

Not sure what to read next? Goodreads allows you to search books by title or author, select certain genres and browse hundreds of books. You can save books that you want to read, rate them and ask the community questions about them as well as write your own reviews. Goodreads also has an app which is available on both iOS and Android.

BookBub

If you're on a budget, don't want to store a library of books in your home but love books, BookBub provides a variety of free ebooks.

Wordery

Wordery is an online bookshop which offer over 10 million books and provide free delivery worldwide.

BookFinder

If you want to purchase your book at the cheapest price, BookFinder compares book prices from over 100,000 booksellers worldwide.

Open Library

The Open Library has more than 1 million free e-books available. It's an open project which allows users to contribute books. You can search by the title, author, and subject.

Apps:

OverDrive

OverDrive lets you buy eBooks and audiobooks without having to leave the house. You never incur late fees as the titles you borrow are automatically returned on their due dates. Over 30,000 libraries are taking part in OverDrive so visit your local library to see if your library card entitles you to use OverDrive on your mobile device.

Google Play Books

Google Play Books is available on Android and iOS. It offers millions of ebooks as well as audio books which you can buy without having a subscription. You can preview the ebooks or listen to free samples of audiobooks before buying them.

Wattpad

Wattpad allows you to read millions of stories as well as write your own ones and share them with other readers.

AnyBooks

AnyBooks is a popular free book app for iPhone and Android that gives access to the latest bestsellers for free. This app allows users to read offline by downloading books. AnyBooks also helps you to translate unknown words instantly by tapping on them.
